# Improved Prompt

You are generating occasion-specific textual anchors for a multimodal fashion recommender that uses text embeddings such as CLIP. Your goal is to create semantically rich, contrastive, and statistically robust text inputs for embedding-based similarity scoring.

Generate outputs for these occasions in this exact order:
- wedding_guest
- party_night_out
- work_office
- formal_event
- casual_everyday
- date_night
- winter_outerwear
- smart_casual
- navidad_dinner
- nochevieja_nye
- halloween
- oktoberfest

For each occasion, produce exactly two sections:

STEP 1 - Conceptual Description
Write exactly one paragraph of 4-6 sentences describing the clothing profile appropriate for that occasion.

Each paragraph must:
- Explicitly define the formality level.
- Describe characteristic silhouettes and garment shapes.
- Mention relevant fabric types, material textures, or surface finishes.
- Describe color tendencies or palette logic.
- Include structural, styling, or layering cues.
- Reflect the social setting and practical expectations of the occasion.
- Use neutral, descriptive language only; no marketing tone, no hype, no aspirational copy.
- Avoid generic phrasing and avoid overlap with other occasions.

Differentiation requirements for the paragraph:
- Make each occasion distinguishable from all others by context, dress code, silhouette logic, material choices, and styling cues.
- Do not let formal_event collapse into wedding_guest.
- Do not let smart_casual collapse into work_office.
- Do not let party_night_out collapse into date_night.
- Do not let navidad_dinner collapse into winter_outerwear.
- Do not let nochevieja_nye collapse into party_night_out.
- Do not let halloween collapse into costume-only language unless the clothing cues remain wearable and fashion-relevant.
- Do not let oktoberfest drift into generic autumn casualwear.

STEP 2 - Embedding Prompts
Convert the paragraph into exactly 5 short prompts for embeddings.

Each prompt must:
- Be 8-15 words long.
- Focus on one primary semantic dimension only.
- Cover five distinct dimensions across the set:
  1. silhouette or shape
  2. fabric or material
  3. formality or dress-code level
  4. structure, styling, or construction
  5. social context, mood, or event behavior
- Use distinct wording within the same occasion.
- Remain clearly tied to the same occasion.
- Avoid brand names, celebrity references, and store-specific language.
- Avoid broad shared phrases across occasions unless strictly unavoidable.

Lexical separation rules:
- Prefer occasion-specific vocabulary instead of reusable fashion clichés.
- Do not repeat phrases like "elegant outfit," "stylish look," "dressy ensemble," or similarly generic wording across multiple occasions.
- Minimize repeated adjectives across different occasions.
- If two occasions are adjacent in formality, separate them using context, garment construction, textile choice, and social function.

Output format for every occasion:
Occasion: <name>
Conceptual Description: <single paragraph>
Embedding Prompts:
- <prompt 1>
- <prompt 2>
- <prompt 3>
- <prompt 4>
- <prompt 5>

Hard constraints:
- No brands.
- No bullet explanations outside the required output format.
- No introductory text and no concluding summary.
- Keep occasion names exactly as listed.
- Ensure all 12 occasions are covered once each.

Quality target:
The result should maximize semantic separability between occasions while preserving internal coherence within each occasion's five prompts.
